Amelia Earhart

Amelia Earhart

Early Life

Earhart is Born

Amelia Mary Earhart was born on July 24, 1897 in Atchison, Kansas. She spent most of her time with her grandparents up until they died. After, her family struggled financially as well with her fathers use of Alcohol.

Earhart Education

The Earharts moved often, and she completed high school in Chicago in 1916. Then Earhart was able to attend the Ogontz School in Rydal, Pennsylvania. However, Amelia developed an interest in caring for soldiers wounded in World War I. In 1918 she left junior college to become a nurse’s aide in Toronto.

Earhart Flies

Shortly after, she went on her first airplane ride in 1920, an experience that prompted her to take flying lessons. In 1921 she bought her first plane, a Kinner Airster, and two years later she earned her pilot’s license.

Accomplishments

Accomplishment #1

On her twenty-fifth birthday, Earhart purchased a Kinner Airster biplane. She flew it, in 1922, when she set the women’s altitude record of 14,000 feet.

Accomplishment #2

In 1932, she became the first woman to fly solo across the Atlantic—as a pilot. Her awards included the American Distinguished Flying Cross and the Cross of the French Legion of Honor.

Accomplishment #3

In 1935, Purdue University hired Earhart as aviation advisor and career counselor for women and purchased the Lockheed plane she dubbed her “flying laboratory.”

Impacts

Earhart was a nurse’s aide.

Earhart became a nurse’s aide with the Red Cross in Toronto and treated wounded soldiers returning from battle during World War I. New York City became her next destination, where she briefly enrolled as a medical student.

First female passenger on a transatlantic flight.

Earhart was the navigator for the pilot and co-pilot. The plane, named Friendship, successfully flew from Canada to Wales. The flight took about 21 hours. Upon her return to the U.S., she was celebrated with a parade in New York City.

First woman to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean.

She was the second person to accomplish this transatlantic journey. Earhart flew from Canada to Ireland in May of 1932, and earned the respect and admiration of aviation enthusiasts around the world.
